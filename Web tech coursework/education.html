Education

A walkthrough of my education journey


Primary School

The Primary school that I attended was Priestmead Primary School. I attended that school from Reception to Year 6, or in other words, from Key stage 0 to Key stage 2. Primary school offers a transition from nursery to the education system and I think that my time in primary school has really shaped who I am as a person.

During primary school I have obtained many skills such as being able to work hard. I have also learnt the importance of taking of my health through eating healthy and working out. I also have gained skills such as reading, writing and basic arithmetic. During school, I studied basic core subjects including Maths, English, Science and humanities.


Secondary School

The Secondary school that I attended was North London Collegiate School. I attended that school from Year 7 to Year 13, or in other words, from Key stage 3 to Key stage 5. Secondary school is a step up from primary school with more exams and work. It allows children to grow and find what they are passionate about.

During secondary school I have obtained 10 GCSEs and an IB diploma. My International Baccalaureate diploma is in the subjects Maths applications and interpretations, English, French, Computer science, Philosophy and Economics. I have a total point score of 40 out of 45. It took a lot of hard work to receive 40 points, and I am grateful to achieve it.


University

The University that I attend is University College London. At UCL I am taking 8 modules in total for my first year. UCL is well-known globally for its contributions to IT and business. UCL also has a rich history and is loacted in London near Euston Square. My lessons are both online and in-person allowing to recive the best of both worlds for my education.

The course that I am taking is Bsc Information Management for business. It is a three year long course. The modules that I take are Foundations of management, Business intelligence, Information systems and Web technologies. My favourite module is Business Intelligence as I enjoy the complexity and analytical nature of the course.
